The clock struck twelve, and the room grew quiet.
Mira opened the notebook and wrote the first line of her machine.
It did not think like a person, but it learned patterns from words.
Each sentence became a trail, and each character became a step.

The model guessed the next mark on the page.
Sometimes it was wrong.
Sometimes it surprised her.
With every pass through the text, the guesses became a little sharper.

By morning, Mira had built a small engine for language.
It was not magic.
It was data, attention, loss, and patience.
